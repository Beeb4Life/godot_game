## Day 4 – Research and Tooling Decisions

### Research
Today I explored how games are structured and what components are typically required to build a complete game. I learned that even simple games include multiple elements such as:

- Main menu
- Gameplay systems
- Sprites and animations
- Backgrounds and environments
- Music and sound effects
- Title screen
- Tutorial or onboarding system

This helped me understand that game development is not just about coding mechanics, but also about integrating multiple assets and systems into a cohesive experience.

### Engine and Tools Consideration
Choosing the right tools is currently one of the most difficult decisions.

#### Options Considered
- GameMaker: Easier to get started with, focused on 2D games
- Godot: Open-source, flexible, and suitable for long-term use
- Libresprite: Free tool for creating sprites
- Aseprite: Paid tool with better usability and features

### Thoughts
- Investing in paid tools like Aseprite could improve workflow but requires consideration
- Godot seems powerful and aligns well with a 2D project
- Still unsure which combination of tools will be the most efficient
